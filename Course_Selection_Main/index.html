Course requirements

Choosing courses

Students are encouraged to read course descriptions and watch the accompanying videos in addition to consulting with their parents and their counsellor before finalizing course selections for next year. In selecting courses, particularly for Grade 10 and 11 students, post-secondary options should be considered. Education Planner BC is a resourceful online site that students can use to help check post secondary requirements in addition to visiting their choice post secondary institution websites.

Ministry Indigenous-focused graduation requirement

All Grade 12s will be taking English 12 First Peoples. This is to meet the new Ministry requirement for graduation which is a minimum of 4 credits in an Indigenous Focused course.

Grade requirements

Grade 9

Students in Grade 9 are required to take a minimum of 9 courses. Please see the Course Planning Sheet for more information. The general courses Grade 9s require are as follows:
  • English 9
  • Mathematics 9
  • Social Studies 9
  • Science 9
  • Physical Education 9
  • 3 electives

Grade 10

Students in Grade 10 are required to take a minimum of 8 courses. The general courses Grade 10s require are as follows:
  • A Language Arts 10
  • Any Mathematics 10
  • Social Studies 10
  • Science 10
  • Career Life Education 10
  • Physical Education 10
  • 2 electives

Grade 11

Students in Grade 11 are required to take a minimum of 8 courses. The general courses Grade 11s require are as follows:
  • A Language Arts 11
  • Any Mathematics 11
  • Any Science 11
  • A Social Studies 12 (may be taken in Grade 11 or 12)
  • Career Life Connections (may be taken in Grade 11 or 12 but Grade 11 is recommended)

Grade 12

Students in Grade 12 are required to take a minimum of 8 courses.
  • English First Peoples 12 (Language Arts 12 requirement)
  • 3 other Grade 12 courses
  • A Social Studies 12 (if you have not taken one in Grade 11)
  • Career Life Connections (if not taken in Grade 11)
The students and staff at Elgin Park Secondary work, learn, and play on the unceded and ancestral territories of the Katzie, Semihamoo, Kwantlen and other Coast Salish Peoples. As responsible members of the Elgin community, we recognize the importance of taking the initiative to learn about and honour the history and traditions of the peoples whose lands we occupy.
