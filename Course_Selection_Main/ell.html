English Language Learning (ELL)

ELL Program Overview

The English Language Learner (ELL) program at Elgin Park is set up on an immersion basis. ELL students are integrated into mainstream classes (academic and/or electives) for all blocks, with the exception of their ELL classes. Placement into the program is determined following the results of their initial placement test prior to starting classes at Elgin Park.

The ELL Levels are:

  1. Level 1 (Beginning)
  2. Level 2 (Developing)
  3. Level 3 (Expanding)
  4. Level 4 (Consolidating)
  5. Level 5 (Bridging)

Level 1 to Level 3 students are usually enrolled in at least one ELL class per semester (direct support).
Level 4 and Level 5 students will typically be in mainstream classes (indirect support).
The ELL classes are divided into three progressive levels. The Level 3 courses will count as 4 credits each.

  • Level 1 - Canadian Culture class
  • Level 2 - Beginner 2 class
  • Level 3 - Introduction to Academic Language ELL 10 - Academic Language ELL 10

Progress through the levels will be dependent upon each student's rate of language acquisition, influenced by factors such as motivation and time in the target language. Advancement is determined partly by annual ELL Testing results, academic class marks, and/or teacher recommendation. ELL students are monitored and tested yearly. Students exiting the ELL program have met criteria such as 5+ years of language instruction in Surrey, achieving Bridging level on assessment and teacher recommendation, or School Based Team approval.

ELL Course Offerings

Level 1 - Canadian Culture

8 9 10 11 12 Elective

Focuses on writing, reading, listening, and speaking English for Beginning level students. Students will learn about Canadian Culture.

Course Code: XSPBK1BE1S

Level 2 - ELL Beginner 2

8 9 10 11 12 Elective

Focuses on writing, reading, listening, and speaking English for Developing level students.

Course Code: XSPBK1AE2S

Level 3 - Introduction to Academic Language ELL 10

8 9 10 11 12 Elective 4 Credits

A bridging course for students at the Expanding level to help prepare them for regular English classes. It focuses on writing, but has components of speaking, listening, reading, and visual representation.

Course Code: YESFL0C--S

Level 3 - Academic Language ELL 10

8 9 10 11 12 Elective 4 Credits

This course has three main objectives: reading, writing, and speaking in English. Students will be exposed to a wide variety of reading genres including both fiction and non-fiction. The focus will be on increasing reading fluency, comprehension, and speed. Students will learn how to respond and decipher various text questions.

Course Code: YESFL0C--S

ELL English 10 / ELL Socials 10

8 9 10 11 12 Elective 4 Credits each

A year-long full credit English 10 course paired with full credit Socials 10 to help develop students' English language acquisition. Pacing of the class will be adjusted for English as a Second Language learners. For full information on English & Socials 10, refer to the English and Socials sections of this website.

Course Code: YESFL0D--S
