Career Education

ELL Program Overview

The goal of career education is to help students reflect and identify their personal interests and skills in order to assist with evaluating the multiple options available to them and ease the transition from secondary school to the workplace and post-secondary school.

Course Offerings

Career Education 8 & 9

8 9 Required

All students receive credit and a letter grade for Career Education 8 & 9. Career-related guest speakers and curriculum will be delivered throughout the year. Participation in and completion of follow-up assignments for Take Our Kids to Work Day for Gr. 9 is required. Both courses are not in the structured timetable and require independent study and initiative from the students.

N/A

Career Life Education 10

10 Required

Career Life Education 10 is a Ministry requirement and a pre-requisite for CLC 12. The aim is to enable students to develop skills to become self-directed individuals who set goals, make thoughtful decisions, and take responsibility for pursuing their goals. It provides opportunities to develop skills, attitudes, and behaviours to manage lives purposefully, enhance personal wellbeing, and realize full potential.

Topics include:

  • Personal Development and Goal Setting
  • Career Research and Networking
  • Interview Skills, Resumes and Cover Letters
  • Wellness and Workplace Safety

Course Code: MCLE-10--S

Career Life Connections 12

11 12 Required

A Ministry Requirement for Graduation, beginning in CLE 10 and culminating with a Capstone presentation. Students must demonstrate:

  • Personal Development: Exploring post-graduation goals/plans; understanding purpose and career-life balance.
  • Connections to Community: Lifelong learning and active citizenship foster opportunities.
  • Career and Life: Complete the Capstone project to present accomplishments and future pathways.

At Elgin, students may take this in grade 11 or 12 (most benefit in grade 11). Offered as a standalone course, through co-op, or paired (e.g., Law/CLC, Environmental Science/CLC).

Course Code: MCLC-12--S
